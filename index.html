Section 3 · What Is at Stake

The Wildlife Paying the Price

Habitat loss directly threatens Malaysia's extraordinary biodiversity. The IUCN Red List tracks species by their risk of extinction — and Malaysia's numbers are alarming.

Threatened Species by Animal Group

Number of Critically Endangered, Endangered, and Vulnerable species in Malaysia by group. Source: IUCN Red List, 2024.

Malaysia vs. ASEAN: Threatened Species Count

How Malaysia compares to neighbouring countries in total threatened species. Source: IUCN Red List, 2024.

Did you know? The Sumatran rhinoceros was declared functionally extinct in Malaysia in 2019 after the last known ividual, Iman, passed away in Sabah. Malaysia now has zero rhinos remaining in the wild.

Section 4 · The Response

Are We Doing Enough?

Malaysia has made commitments to conservation — including the Ecological Fiscal Transfer (EFT), which channels government funds to states for biodiversity protection. But how much is actually being spent, and is it working?

The accountability gap. Macaranga's 2025 investigation found that of RM 517 million disbursed since 2019, the vast majority had no public documentation of how the funds were spent. Only four states — Johor, Sabah, Pahang, and Kelantan — provided any spending breakdown at all.
